# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Lockd is for Gen Z and young adult iPhone users who feel pulled into TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, Reddit, Safari, and similar high-drift apps when they are trying to study, work, sleep, or reset their attention. They arrive from a moment of frustration: too much screen time, failed Screen Time limits, a desire to "lock in", or curiosity about monk mode and dopamine detox content.

## Product Purpose

Lockd helps users protect attention before distraction starts. It combines native app blocking, soft friction, local weak-spot prediction, Focus Score feedback, and shareable recap cards. Success means users can start focus sessions quickly, trust the app to intervene at risky moments, and feel proud of reclaimed time without feeling shamed by the product.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, controlled, humane. Lockd should market with competitive urgency and cultural edge, then feel calm and respectful inside the app. The brand can speak in the language of lock-in culture, but daily product copy should make the user feel protected rather than judged.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic wellness calm that loses the Gen Z wedge, gamer-dark overload, AI-purple gradient sameness, fake science around dopamine, shame-based failure copy, manipulative streak anxiety, cluttered productivity dashboards, and any interface that makes bypassing or leaving feel hidden.

## Design Principles

- Sell the transformation sharply, then make the product experience humane.
- Make the primary action obvious: protect the next block of time.
- Explain predictions clearly so "AI" feels trustworthy, not magical.
- Keep Focus Score private by default and sharing opt-in.
- Treat failure as recovery, not punishment.
- Use native iOS patterns, haptics, and accessibility before decorative novelty.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast where applicable, full Dynamic Type support, VoiceOver labels for all score, timer, and prediction states, 44pt or larger touch targets, reduced-motion alternatives for every animation, non-color status cues for grades and warnings, and clear controls for notifications, blocking rules, sharing, and subscription cancellation.
