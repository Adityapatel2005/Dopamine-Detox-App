# Lockd Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 18, 2026

Lockd is built as a local-first focus app. In the current v1 design, Focus Score, selected distracting apps, lock sessions, weak-spot predictions, goals, streaks, and recap content are processed on device and are not sent to Lockd servers.

This policy is a product baseline for review before public launch. The public App Store version must use a hosted privacy policy URL that matches the final app behavior.

## Information Lockd Handles

- Screen Time app selections: app and category choices you select for blocking or friction.
- Focus sessions: start time, duration, selected rules, and completion state.
- Focus Score inputs: on-device session and rule signals used to estimate progress.
- Weak-spot predictions: on-device patterns that help identify high-risk times.
- Recap cards: optional summaries generated locally and shared only when you choose.
- Purchase status: subscription status handled through Apple In-App Purchase once configured.

## Information Collected by Lockd

The current v1 scaffold is designed to collect no personal data on Lockd servers. It does not include advertising SDKs, third-party analytics SDKs, cross-app tracking, or data broker integrations.

## How Information Is Used

Lockd uses on-device information to provide app blocking flows, focus session feedback, weak-spot prediction, subscription gating, and private recap experiences. Lockd does not sell personal information or share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising.

## Children

Lockd is not intended for children under 13 and should not be submitted in Apple's Kids Category. If the product later supports teen or family use cases, the team must complete a child privacy review, including COPPA and App Store Kids Category requirements where applicable.

## Legal Rights

Depending on where you live, including under the GDPR, California privacy laws, and similar privacy rules, you may have rights to access, delete, correct, export, or restrict certain personal information. Because the v1 app is local-first, most app data can be controlled by deleting local data or uninstalling the app.

## California Notice

Lockd does not sell personal information or share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising in the current v1 design. California residents may request information about data practices, deletion, correction, and non-discrimination rights through the support channel published with the app.

## GDPR Notice

For GDPR purposes, the public release must identify the controller, lawful bases, support contact, and any processors before launch. The v1 design relies on on-device processing and data minimization. Any future backend, analytics, email, or support system must be added to the data map before release.

## Data Retention

Local app data remains on your device until you delete it, reset the app, or uninstall Lockd. Purchase records are managed by Apple under Apple's App Store terms.

## Changes

Lockd should update this policy whenever data handling, vendors, analytics, account features, subscriptions, or sharing behavior changes.
